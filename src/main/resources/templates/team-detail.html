The Chinese women's curling team was founded in 2003. Although the establishment time was short, the Chinese curling girls were among the top teams in the world in only 5 or 6 years. In 2007, the Chinese team hired Canadian foreign teacher Daniel. The Chinese women's curling performance can be described as rapid progress. In the Women's Curling World Championships in 2008, the Chinese girls won the runner-up, and on March 25, 2009, they won the World Championship of Women's World Curling Championship and won the World Cup Vancouver 2010.Bronze medals, with a historical breakthrough in this department,they eventually won the 2009 Best Team Award for Sportsman of the Year Awarded by China Central Television.
